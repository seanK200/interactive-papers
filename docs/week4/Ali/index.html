A certain priest [Giovanni da Carigano], the rector[2] of St. Mark in Genoa, a truly excellent man, published a treatise, which he called a ‘map’. Among many things written in it about the state of this nation [the Ethiopians] he reports[4] that Prester John is set over that people as patriarch; and he says that under him [the patriarch] are 127 bishoprics, each of which has twenty bishops. Those who are to be reborn they baptize in the Roman manner, In the name of the Father and the Son and the Holy Spirit; and in the same way they celebrate the Sacrament of the Eucharist, with this one exception, that they sing the Paternoster before the elevation of the Sacrament. And after the Blessed Virgin Mary they hold in peculiar honour the apostles Peter and Paul and John the Baptist, also the other apostles, and specially do they venerate with great devotion Matthew their principal teacher.
