Of the Ethiopians there are divers forms and kinds of men. Some there are toward the east that have neither nose nor nostrils, but the face all full. Others that have no upper lip, they are without tongues, and they speak by signs, and they have but a little hole to take their breath at, by the which they drink with an oaten straw. There are some called Syrbote that are eight foot high, they live with the chase of elephants. In a part of Affricke be people called Ptoemphane, for their king they have a dog, at whose fancy they are governed . . .
